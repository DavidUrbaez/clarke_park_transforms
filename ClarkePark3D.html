Clarke & Park, as geometry

The white ball is the instantaneous point (ia, ib, ic) plotted in 3-D abc-space. Balanced three-phase signals never leave the tilted disc — the plane a+b+c=0. Clarke just renames axes: α, β span that plane, 0 is the axis perpendicular to it. Park rotates with the vector so it stands still.
  • ▶ look down 0-axis: the a, b, c axes land 120° apart — the classic space-vector diagram.
  • Toggle Park (dq) frame: the vector freezes, the abc axes spin backwards.
  • Add negative seq: circle → ellipse, and d, q wobble at 2ω.
  • Add zero seq: the ball finally leaves the plane, sliding along the purple 0-axis.
  • Add 5th harmonic: a lobed orbit, a 6ω ripple in dq.
